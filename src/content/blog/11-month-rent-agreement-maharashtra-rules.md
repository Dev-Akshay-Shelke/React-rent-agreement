## Why 11 Months? The Legal Reason

The preference for 11-month rent agreements is deeply rooted in Indian property law. Under the Transfer of Property Act, 1882, a lease of immovable property for a period of 12 months or more must be registered compulsorily. Agreements for periods less than 12 months were historically exempt from registration.

To avoid the cost and complexity of registration, landlords began drafting agreements for 11 months — just one month short of the mandatory threshold.

However, in Maharashtra, this exemption no longer fully applies. The **Maharashtra Rent Control Act requires registration of Leave & License agreements regardless of duration.** Despite this, the 11-month convention remains the standard across Maharashtra and the rest of India due to tradition and ease of periodic renewal.

## Is Registration Mandatory for an 11-Month Agreement in Maharashtra?

**Yes.** Under the Maharashtra Rent Control Act, 1999, Section 55, registration of a Leave & License agreement is mandatory, regardless of whether the period is 11 months or shorter. An unregistered agreement is not admissible as evidence in a court of law.

This is a critical point many landlords overlook. An unregistered 11-month agreement offers no legal protection — it is treated as void for the purposes of resolving disputes. Both landlord and tenant are legally exposed.

## Key Terms of an 11-Month Leave & License Agreement

A properly drafted 11-month agreement should include:

- Full names and addresses of Licensor (landlord) and Licensee (tenant)
- Complete address and description of the licensed premises
- Monthly licence fee (rent) amount
- Security deposit amount and refund conditions
- Duration: specific start and end date (11 months)
- Lock-in period if applicable
- Maintenance and utility responsibility
- Conditions for early termination
- Notice period requirement
- Renewal terms

## What Happens When the 11-Month Period Expires?

When an 11-month Leave & License agreement expires, the tenancy technically ends. The tenant does not automatically acquire tenancy rights. The landlord and tenant have three options:

1. **Renew the agreement** by executing a new Leave & License agreement (most common)
2. Allow the tenant to vacate the premises
3. Execute a fresh longer-term agreement if both parties agree

It is important to renew the agreement before expiry. If a tenant continues to occupy the property after the agreement expires without a new agreement, it can create a complex legal situation under Rent Control provisions.

## Benefits of Registering Your 11-Month Agreement

Registration of your Leave & License agreement provides:

**Legal Admissibility:** A registered agreement is valid evidence in any court. You can file a complaint for eviction or unpaid rent with the document as proof.

**Defined Termination Rights:** A registered agreement allows landlords to recover possession more efficiently through the notary process under the Maharashtra Rent Control Act.

**Tenant Protection:** Tenants are protected from arbitrary eviction before the agreement period ends.

**Dispute Prevention:** Clear, registered terms prevent disputes about rent amounts, deposit refunds, and maintenance responsibilities.

**Official Proof of Address:** A registered rent agreement is accepted as proof of address by banks, government offices, passport authorities, and educational institutions.
