## The Simple Answer: They Are Not the Same

In everyday conversation, landlords and tenants in Pune and Mumbai use "rent agreement" to describe any document governing a property letting. However, in Maharashtra law, there is a **critical distinction** between a Lease/Rental Agreement and a Leave & License Agreement.

This distinction has serious legal consequences for property recovery, tenant rights, and dispute resolution.

## What Is a Leave & License Agreement?

A Leave & License Agreement is governed by **Section 52 of the Indian Easements Act, 1882**. Under this arrangement:

- The licensor (property owner) grants the licensee (tenant/occupant) the right to use the property for a specific period
- **No interest in the property is transferred** to the licensee
- The licensee is occupying the property with the licensor's permission only
- The licensor retains full ownership and possession rights at all times
- The license is personal and cannot be transferred or sub-licensed

This is the standard, legally preferred document used in Maharashtra for residential and commercial lettings.

## What Is a Rental or Lease Agreement?

A Rental or Lease Agreement is governed by the **Transfer of Property Act, 1882**. Under a lease:

- The landlord transfers the right to use and enjoy the property to the tenant for a specific period
- The tenant acquires a **property right** (called a leasehold interest) in the property
- This interest can be transferred or sub-let unless the agreement prohibits it
- The tenant has stronger occupation rights, making eviction significantly more difficult
- A lease for 12 months or more must be registered

Because leases confer property rights on tenants, they make it harder for landlords to recover possession and are rarely used for residential lettings in Maharashtra today.

## Why Leave & License Is Preferred in Maharashtra

Maharashtra landlords almost exclusively use Leave & License agreements because:

**No Transfer of Property Rights:** Since no property interest passes to the licensee, the landlord can recover possession at the end of the licence period or upon breach of terms without going through lengthy eviction proceedings.

**Faster Recovery:** Under the Maharashtra Rent Control Act, landlords with a registered Leave & License agreement can recover possession through the Executive Magistrate process, which is faster than civil courts.

**Clear Termination:** The agreement ends automatically at the expiry date, after giving the prescribed notice period.

**Legal Recognition:** Registered Leave & License agreements are fully recognized by Maharashtra courts and government authorities.

## Registration Is Mandatory for Both

Whether you execute a Leave & License agreement or a lease/rental agreement, registration is mandatory in Maharashtra.

- **For Leave & License:** Required under Section 55 of the Maharashtra Rent Control Act, 1999, regardless of duration.
- **For Lease:** Required under the Registration Act, 1908, for any lease exceeding 11 months.

An unregistered agreement of either type is not admissible as evidence in court proceedings and provides no legal protection to either party.
