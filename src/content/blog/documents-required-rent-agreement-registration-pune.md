## Documents Required from the Landlord (Licensor)

The property owner needs to provide the following documents:

**Identity & Address Proof (any one):**
- Aadhaar Card (mandatory for biometric e-KYC)
- PAN Card
- Passport or Driving Licence

**Property Ownership Documents (any one):**
- Property registration document / Sale Deed
- Index 2 of the property
- Society share certificate (for housing society flats)
- Property tax receipt in the owner's name
- Electricity bill in the owner's name

**Photograph:** Recent passport-size photograph (soft copy acceptable for online registration)

**Bank Details (optional but recommended):** Account details for security deposit transactions

## Documents Required from the Tenant (Licensee)

The tenant needs to provide:

**Identity & Address Proof (mandatory):**
- Aadhaar Card (mandatory for biometric e-KYC verification)
- PAN Card

**Photograph:** Recent passport-size photograph (soft copy acceptable)

**For Corporate or Business Tenants:**
- Company PAN card and GST registration
- Board resolution authorizing the signatory
- Certificate of Incorporation
- Authorized signatory's Aadhaar and PAN

## Documents for Co-Owners or Joint Property

If the property has multiple owners, all co-owners must participate in the registration process. Each co-owner needs to provide their Aadhaar card and PAN card. All co-owners must consent to the licence arrangement.

Alternatively, one co-owner may authorize another through a registered Power of Attorney to represent them in the agreement. Our team at Prime Document Solutions will guide you through this process if your property has joint ownership.

## Documents for Properties in Housing Societies

For flats or properties within registered cooperative housing societies, additional documents may be required:

- **Society NOC** (No Objection Certificate) — though not always mandatory, many societies require this
- Society share certificate
- Maintenance dues clearance certificate from the society

It is advisable to check with your housing society's office before proceeding with registration to ensure compliance with society bye-laws.
