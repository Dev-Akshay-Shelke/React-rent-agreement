## What Is Stamp Duty on a Rent Agreement?

Stamp duty is a government tax levied on legal documents to make them legally valid and admissible in court. In Maharashtra, a rent agreement (also called a Leave & License Agreement) must be stamped and registered to be legally enforceable under the Maharashtra Stamp Act, 1958.

Without proper stamp duty payment and registration, the agreement cannot be used as evidence in any legal proceeding, leaving both landlord and tenant legally unprotected.

## Stamp Duty Rates in Maharashtra (2024–25)

The stamp duty on a Leave & License agreement in Maharashtra is calculated based on the average monthly rent and the duration of the agreement.

The applicable rate is **0.25% of the total rent payable** for the entire licence period plus the security deposit.

**Example Calculation:**
- Monthly Rent: ₹15,000
- Duration: 11 months
- Security Deposit: ₹30,000
- Total Rent: ₹15,000 × 11 = ₹1,65,000
- Total Consideration (Rent + Deposit): ₹1,65,000 + ₹30,000 = ₹1,95,000
- Stamp Duty @ 0.25%: ₹487.50 (rounded to ₹500)

The minimum stamp duty applicable is ₹500 and is the same for both residential and commercial property.

## Stamp Duty for Commercial vs Residential Properties

The stamp duty rate is the same (0.25%) for both residential and commercial Leave & License agreements in Maharashtra. However, commercial agreements often involve higher rents, CAM charges, and security deposits, which increase the overall stamp duty amount.

For commercial properties, it is important to include all recurring charges in the total consideration to avoid disputes about underpayment of stamp duty.

## Registration Charges in Addition to Stamp Duty

Besides stamp duty, a registration fee is also payable when registering a rent agreement. The registration fee in Maharashtra is **1% of the total licence amount**, subject to a maximum of ₹30,000.

For most standard residential agreements (under 5 years), the total cost including stamp duty + registration + our service fee remains very affordable and provides complete legal protection.

## Why Underpaying Stamp Duty Is Risky

If stamp duty is underpaid on a rent agreement, the document is treated as insufficiently stamped and can be impounded. A penalty of **up to 10 times the deficient stamp duty** can be imposed under the Maharashtra Stamp Act.

More importantly, an improperly stamped agreement cannot be used as evidence in civil disputes, leaving the landlord unable to claim unpaid rent or the tenant unable to prove occupation rights.
